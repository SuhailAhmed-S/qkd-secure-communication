The Quantum Advantage

What is Quantum Key Distribution?

QKD leverages quantum mechanics to distribute encryption keys with proven information-theoretic security. Unlike classical cryptography, which relies on computational hardness, QKD is unbreakable by principle—any eavesdropping attempt introduces detectable errors.

Our BB84 implementation demonstrates this revolutionary approach with real-time eavesdropping detection.

Eavesdropping Detection

The quantum channel betrays any unauthorized access. When Eve intercepts qubits, she introduces measurement errors that reveal her presence through elevated QBER (Quantum Bit Error Rate).

The Quantum Pipeline

1

Qubit Preparation

Alice randomly generates bits and encodes them into quantum states using two bases: rectilinear (+) and diagonal (×).

random_bits → random_bases → quantum_states
2

Quantum Transmission

Encoded qubits are sent through the quantum channel. Eve may intercept (optional), but any measurement causes quantum collapse.

quantum_channel → [eve_optional] → bob
3

Measurement & Sifting

Bob measures each qubit in a random basis. Only qubits where Bob's basis matches Alice's are kept (sifted key).

bob_bases → sift_key (~50% retained)
4

QBER Analysis

Calculate Quantum Bit Error Rate to detect eavesdropping. QBER < 11% = secure, > 11% = abort (Eve detected).

QBER = errors / total_bits
5

Privacy Amplification

Apply universal hashing (Toeplitz matrices) and SHA-3 extraction to compress the key and eliminate information leakage.

toeplitz_hash(sifted_key) → final_key
6

Secure Communication

The final key is used for XOR-based encryption. Message is encrypted and only Bob (with the matching key) can decrypt it.

ciphertext = message XOR final_key
